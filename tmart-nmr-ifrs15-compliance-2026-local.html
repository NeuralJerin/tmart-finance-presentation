Supplier income categories booked monthly under NMR — split into contract-based items calculated on net purchase (including logistic rebates for warehouse-delivered POs) and marketing activities performed by suppliers during the month.

KPMG Audit Concern
THE ISSUE

KPMG flagged that NMR revenue was being recognized based on a percentage of net purchase without validating whether a real service was actually provided to the supplier — a direct violation of IFRS 15.

❌ Old Practice

STEP 1
→
Supplier contract defines NMR as a % of net purchase
STEP 2
→
Revenue recognized automatically based on purchase volume
STEP 3
→
No validation of whether a service was actually provided
Result: Revenue recognized for unutilized services — IFRS violation flagged

Why This Was a Problem

Unutilized Revenue

NMR income was recognized based on purchase volumes without confirming any service was delivered to the supplier — booking revenue for activities that may never have occurred.

KPMG Audit Risk

KPMG formally raised this concern, creating material audit exposure. Continued non-compliance would risk qualified audit opinions and regulatory scrutiny.

Core Issue
Revenue was booked on net purchase percentage alone — no validation of service delivery — resulting in recognition of unutilized revenue across NMR categories

KPMG Recommendation Implemented
THE FIX

What Was Wrong

  • NMR recognized as a % of net purchase automatically — no service validation
  • No evidence required to prove an activity was actually performed for the supplier
  • Revenue booked for unutilized services — direct IFRS 15 violation
  • P&L reflected income that didn't correspond to real service delivery
  • KPMG flagged the practice as a material compliance concern

What We Fixed

  • Revenue recognized only when evidence of activity fulfillment is submitted
  • Communicated new policy to commercial team per KPMG recommendation
  • No NMR income booked unless proof of service delivery is provided to the supplier
  • End-to-end revenue recognition cycle now fully IFRS 15 compliant
  • P&L now accurately reflects what is happening on the ground for revenue fulfillment
H1 2026 Process Enhancements
Revenue Recognition

Evidence-Based Booking

NMR revenue is no longer recognized on net purchase percentage alone. Income is booked only after evidence is submitted confirming that the activity has been fulfilled and provided to the supplier.

Before
Auto-recognized on net purchase % — no service validation
After
Recognized only with evidence of activity fulfillment
Impact & Compliance

Margin Impact Acknowledged

The new process will impact margins as unutilized revenue is no longer recognized. However, this ensures a fully compliant revenue recognition cycle and a P&L that accurately reflects real activity on the ground.

Before
Inflated margins from unutilized revenue
After
True margins reflecting actual service delivery
